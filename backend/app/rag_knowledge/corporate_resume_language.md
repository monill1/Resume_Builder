# Corporate Resume Language

Corporate resume feedback should be direct, practical, and truthful. Avoid overclaiming. Prefer evidence-based edits over generic motivational advice.

Strong resume language uses action verbs such as built, improved, automated, analyzed, led, reduced, increased, deployed, designed, implemented, optimized, collaborated, and delivered.

For each important claim, ask whether the resume shows:

- What was done
- Which tool or method was used
- What business or user problem it affected
- What measurable result, scope, or output was created

Good phrasing:

- "Built a reporting dashboard for weekly operations reviews" is stronger than "Good knowledge of dashboards."
- "Automated invoice reconciliation using Excel formulas and Python scripts" is stronger than "Worked on Excel and Python."
- "Collaborated with product and QA teams to release customer-facing features" is stronger than "Team player."

When evidence is missing, suggest a truthful next step: add a real project, add a clearer bullet, move a skill into a relevant section, or keep the keyword out until the candidate can support it.
