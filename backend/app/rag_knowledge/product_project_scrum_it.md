# IT Product Manager Project Manager and Scrum Master Expectations

IT product, project, and scrum resumes should show delivery ownership, prioritization, roadmap planning, stakeholder alignment, Agile ceremonies, risk management, and measurable outcomes.

Common signals include roadmap, backlog, user stories, sprint planning, release planning, Jira, Confluence, Scrum, Kanban, stakeholder management, prioritization, OKRs, KPIs, risk tracking, dependency management, UAT, go-live, and change management.

Hiring teams value shipped outcomes, cross-functional coordination, decision quality, and ability to unblock teams.

Good product/project bullets:

- Managed sprint planning, backlog refinement, and release tracking across product, engineering, QA, and business teams.
- Prioritized roadmap items using user impact, business value, effort, and dependency analysis.
- Reduced delivery risk by tracking blockers, clarifying scope, and aligning stakeholders on release readiness.
- Coordinated UAT and go-live activities, ensuring defects, documentation, and stakeholder sign-off were complete.

Weak bullets only say "managed team." Strong bullets show scope, method, decisions, risk, delivery, and measurable business or product result.
