# ATS Formatting Rules

ATS parsers work best with selectable plain text, standard section headings, consistent dates, and simple reading order.

Avoid tables, text boxes, heavy icons, skill bars, image-only contact details, and unusual section titles when the resume needs to pass automated screening.

Use standard headings such as Summary, Skills, Experience, Projects, Education, and Certifications. Keep email, phone, location, LinkedIn, GitHub, and portfolio links near the top as plain text.

Use one date format throughout the resume, preferably Month YYYY or YYYY. Mark current roles as Present. Avoid mixing full dates, month-year, and year-only formats.

Strong ATS bullets usually include action, tool, scope, and outcome. A good bullet explains what the candidate did, what technology or method was used, and what changed because of the work.

Do not add unsupported keywords. If the candidate has not used a skill, suggest learning or project work instead of inserting fake experience.

Keyword stuffing reduces trust. Use job description terms naturally in summary, skills, projects, and experience bullets.
