# IT Business Analyst Market Expectations

IT business analyst resumes should show requirements gathering, stakeholder communication, process mapping, documentation, user stories, acceptance criteria, UAT, and delivery coordination.

Common signals include BRD, FRD, user stories, acceptance criteria, Jira, Confluence, Agile, Scrum, process mapping, gap analysis, stakeholder management, wireframes, SQL basics, reporting, UAT, change requests, and product documentation.

Hiring teams expect proof that the candidate can translate business needs into clear technical requirements and reduce delivery confusion.

Good IT business analyst bullets:

- Gathered requirements from business stakeholders and converted them into user stories with acceptance criteria for development teams.
- Created process maps and gap analysis to clarify current-state issues and proposed system improvements.
- Coordinated UAT by preparing test scenarios, tracking defects, and confirming business sign-off before release.
- Maintained Jira and Confluence documentation to keep scope, decisions, and delivery status transparent.

Weak BA bullets only say "communicated with stakeholders." Strong bullets show artifacts created, decisions clarified, and delivery risk reduced.
