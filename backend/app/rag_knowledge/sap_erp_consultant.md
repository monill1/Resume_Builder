# SAP and ERP Consultant Market Expectations

SAP and ERP consultant resumes should show module knowledge, business process understanding, configuration, documentation, testing, support, and stakeholder coordination.

Common signals include SAP FICO, MM, SD, HCM, ABAP, S/4HANA, ECC, procurement, inventory, sales order, finance, master data, configuration, functional specification, support tickets, UAT, data migration, integration, and training.

Hiring teams expect evidence that the candidate understands business processes and can translate them into ERP configuration, support, or reporting outcomes.

Good SAP ERP bullets:

- Supported SAP module configuration and issue resolution for business users, documenting root cause and corrective action.
- Prepared functional specifications and coordinated with technical teams for enhancements or reports.
- Assisted UAT by creating scenarios, validating process flows, and tracking defects through closure.
- Maintained master data or transaction process documentation to reduce recurring user errors.

Weak ERP bullets only list module names. Strong bullets show business process, configuration/support action, stakeholder coordination, and outcome.
