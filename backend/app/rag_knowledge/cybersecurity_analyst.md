# Cybersecurity Analyst Market Expectations

Cybersecurity resumes should show risk detection, incident response, vulnerability management, security monitoring, compliance awareness, and clear documentation.

Common signals include SIEM, SOC, Splunk, QRadar, Microsoft Sentinel, vulnerability assessment, penetration testing, OWASP, Nmap, Burp Suite, Wireshark, IDS/IPS, firewalls, IAM, MFA, endpoint security, phishing analysis, ISO 27001, SOC 2, GDPR, and incident reporting.

Hiring teams value evidence of investigation quality, triage, risk reduction, controls, and communication with technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Good cybersecurity bullets:

- Monitored SIEM alerts, triaged suspicious activity, and escalated confirmed incidents with documented evidence and severity.
- Performed vulnerability scans, prioritized findings by risk, and coordinated remediation with infrastructure teams.
- Analyzed phishing emails and endpoint alerts to identify indicators of compromise and reduce user risk.
- Created incident reports summarizing timeline, affected assets, root cause, containment, and prevention steps.

Weak security bullets only list tools. Strong bullets show how the candidate detected, investigated, remediated, or prevented risk.
